About


The Product:

The keeper teacher is an autonomous soccer ball launching mechanism designed for use in keeper training drills.


The Need:

In order to validate the need for the keeper teacher, we conducted a shooting accuracy study involving casual soccer players aged 20-22.
Data was gathered on shot placement, keeper skill and accuracy on shots taken 18 yards from the net. From this, we found that:

  • Roughly 35% of shots missed the net when attempting to score on a goalie
  • Of the saves the goalkeeper made, 88% resulted in rebounds
  • Shooting accuracy on a 1x1m target was an average of 25%



Inaccurate shooting prevents keepers from recieving consistent training.

The keeper teacher will be designed to consistently and accurately deliver balls for use in repetitive drills.



Specifications:

We want to create a low-cost, easily operable keeper training mechanism with the following characteristics:

  • Able to shoot soccer balls autonomously from at least 18 yards with reasonable pace
  • Holds at least 3 balls
  • Shoots with a frequency of at least 0.25 Hz
  • Able to hit a 1x1m target 18 yards away 90% of the time
  • Powered by wall outlet or battery with at least 1 hour battery life
  • Operable in light rain

Design

Preliminary Design:

  • Fly wheels for ball launching
  • Capable of adjustable pitch and yaw - changes ball launch direction

Secondary Design:

  • Flexible wheels for increased pressure on ball
  • Linear actuator for pitch - high accuracy needed for small angle changes
  • Turn table at bottom for yaw - support brackets to stabalize during rotation
